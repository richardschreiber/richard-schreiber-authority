# The Autism Parent
## ICA Avatar | AI Stewardship | Richard Schreiber

---

## CANONICAL NAME
The Autism Parent

## WHO THEY ARE
Parent of an autistic child, ages 8-22. Relying on Medicaid as primary or secondary payer for behavioral therapy. Navigating IEP systems, therapy authorizations, and insurance denials simultaneously. Medically literate but legally underequipped. Exhausted and paying attention.

## THEIR MOMENT
Medicaid waiver application: status unknown. ABA therapy authorization: expires in 60 days. Provider called: their billing authorization is under review. Congressional representative: has not called back. Transition plan: lists goals but names no funded programs.

## THEIR FEAR
Services will be cut before their child is ready. They will discover the services cliff only when they are already standing at the edge. They will not know how to fight until it is too late to fight.

## WHAT THEY NEED FROM RICHARD
Specific actions this week — not policy analysis. Plain language on what Medicaid actually covers and what is at risk. The Services Cliff Framework — named and mapped — so they can see it before they fall off it. Permission to call their congressional representative instead of emailing.

## WHAT THEY DO NOT NEED
Academic research on autism prevalence. Content assuming professional advocacy experience. Hope without a ladder.

## PRIMARY TRACK AND PLATFORMS
Autism advocacy content. Primary platforms: Autism Care Revolution (book), Substack, LinkedIn, foundation site.

## HOW AGENTS USE THIS AVATAR
- Stephen / Maya / Isaac: Use the Services Cliff Framework and Audit Protection Framework explicitly. Structure every piece around the Five Next Steps Model. Close on agency, never alarm. Use plain, legally precise language about Medicaid, IDEA, and waivers. Never invent statistics; source every number (e.g., "40% rely on Medicaid," "5-10 year waitlists"). Maintain the tone of a practitioner who has navigated the system for 19 years firsthand.
- Voice Check: Does this hand them a ladder before the edge, or just point at the drop? If it points at the drop, rewrite.
