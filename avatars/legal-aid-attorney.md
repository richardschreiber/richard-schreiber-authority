# The Legal Aid Managing Attorney
## ICA Avatar | AI Stewardship | Richard Schreiber

---

## CANONICAL NAME
The Legal Aid Managing Attorney

## WHO THEY ARE
Managing attorney at a legal aid society, public defender office, or access-to-justice nonprofit. Staff of 5-25 attorneys. No dedicated IT staff. Annual budget under $5 million. Client base: undocumented immigrants, domestic violence survivors, families in housing court.

## THEIR MOMENT
A ransomware note arrived on Tuesday. 1,200 client files are now in criminal hands. The question is no longer whether to pay. The question is how to tell 1,200 clients.

## PROFESSIONAL FEAR
A data breach exposes the location of a domestic violence survivor. A staff attorney files a brief with fabricated AI citations and the judge makes an example. An OIG audit freezes their provider's Medicaid authorization and their clients lose services.

## PERSONAL FEAR
They will fail the people who rely on them most because the system was underfunded and they didn't know how to lock the doors in time.

## WHAT THEY NEED FROM RICHARD
Free or near-free security controls implementable this week. A written AI use policy draftable in one afternoon. The ABA Opinion 512 framing — because professional responsibility is the language they already speak. Confirmation that governance is not a luxury for well-funded organizations.

## WHAT THEY DO NOT NEED
Enterprise cybersecurity pricing. Content assuming a dedicated compliance officer. Theory without a Tuesday morning action list.

## PRIMARY TRACK AND PLATFORMS
SecureJustice.ai cybersecurity content. Primary platforms: securejustice.ai, Substack.

## HOW AGENTS USE THIS AVATAR
- Stephen / Maya / Isaac: Lead with professional responsibility and survival infrastructure. Emphasize the Low-Budget Cyber Hygiene Framework and the Four AI Stewardship Practices. Use exact, verbatim statistics (e.g., "$49.5M in nonprofit breach settlements"). Never frame cybersecurity as an enterprise luxury; frame it as a moral and professional obligation to vulnerable clients. Close with immediate, $0-cost actions.
- Voice Check: Does this match the stakes of a Tuesday morning ransomware note? If it reads like a corporate compliance memo, rewrite it for the people who actually hold the files.
